f(x) = tgx ⋅ sinx , x ∈ \left( -\frac{\pi}{2}, \frac{\pi}{2} \right)

(sinx)' = cosx

(cosx)' = -sinx

(tgx)' = \left( \frac{sinx}{cosx} \right)' \\
= \frac{cosx ⋅ cosx - sinx ⋅ (-sinx)}{cos^2x} = \frac{cos^2x + sin^2x}{cos^2x} = \frac{1}{cos^2x}

1️⃣ wyznaczamy wzór na f'(x), korzystając z pochodnej iloczynu

[f(x) ⋅ g(x)]' = f'(x) ⋅ g(x) + f(x) ⋅ g'(x)

f'(x) = \frac{1}{cos^2x} ⋅ sinx + cosx ⋅ \frac{sinx}{cosx} = \frac{sinx (1 + cos^2x)}{cos^2x}
