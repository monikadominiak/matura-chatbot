Oblicz granicę lim_{n \to \infty}(n - \sqrt{n+3}).

📝 Obliczamy granicę.

lim_{n \to \infty}(n - \sqrt{n+3}) = lim_{n \to \infty} n \left(1 - \sqrt{1 + \frac{3}{n}}\right) = lim_{n \to \infty} n(1 - 0) = lim_{n \to \infty} n = \infty

\lim_{n \to \infty} \sqrt{n+3} = \lim_{n \to \infty} \sqrt{n} \cdot \sqrt{1+\frac{3}{n}} = \lim_{n \to \infty} n^{\frac{1}{2}} \sqrt{1+\frac{3}{n}} = 0,

Odp.: \lim_{n \to \infty} (n - \sqrt{n+3}) = \infty.
